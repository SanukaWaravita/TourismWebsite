Explore the Rich Culture of Sri Lanka

A journey through our traditions, heritage and vibrant way of life.

Religious Diversity

Buddhism

A major religion in Sri Lanka, known for its rich history and cultural significance.

Hinduism

Rich traditions, vibrant festivals and colorful Kovils around the island.

Islam

Peaceful communities with beautiful Mosques and unique customs.

Christianity

Churches and colonial history shaping the Christian faith in Sri Lanka.

Festivals & Celebrations

Sinhala and Tamil New Year

April's vibrant festival marks the sun's movement from Pisces to Aries, celebrated with rituals, food, and traditional games.

Vesak Festival

Celebrating the birth, enlightenment, and death of Buddha with lanterns and religious observances.

Kandy Esala Perahera

A grand procession held in Kandy, featuring traditional dancers, drummers, and beautifully decorated elephants.

Thai Pongal

A harvest festival celebrated by Tamils, featuring rice dishes and cultural events.

Christmas

Celebrated joyfully with decorated homes, midnight masses, and feasts, spreading love and peace across communities.

Eid-ul-Fitr

A joyous occasion for Muslims after Ramadan, marked by prayer, festive meals, and acts of generosity.

Ancient Architecture

Sigiriya Rock Fortress

An ancient rock palace and fortress with frescoes and water gardens, built by King Kashyapa.

Polonnaruwa Ruins

A UNESCO World Heritage Site showcasing royal palaces, temples, and Buddha statues.

Ruwanwelisaya

A massive stupa in Anuradhapura built by King Dutugemunu, sacred to Buddhists.

Gal Viharaya

A collection of majestic rock-carved Buddha statues in Polonnaruwa.

Yapahuwa Fortress

A rock fortress with a dramatic stone staircase, once a royal capital.

Jetavanaramaya

One of the tallest ancient brick structures in the world, located in Anuradhapura.

Sri Lankan Cuisine

Rice and Curry

A flavorful dish of rice served with a variety of spicy curries, vegetables, and sambols.

Kottu Roti

A popular street food made from chopped roti stir-fried with vegetables, eggs, and spices.

Hoppers

Bowl-shaped pancakes made from fermented rice flour and coconut milk.

String Hoppers

Steamed rice noodle discs served with coconut sambol and spicy gravy.

Curd and Treacle

A traditional dessert made with buffalo curd and palm treacle.

Lamprais

A flavorful dish of rice, meat, and vegetables wrapped in a banana leaf and baked.
